| **Intimate**      | Live music in the room, adapting in real time to the ceremony's pace.            |

## Signature Sound — Piano Paired with Strings

Allegra's identity is **piano paired with strings**: piano holds the harmony and rhythm, violin
and cello carry the melody. Formations scale from piano + solo violin to piano + full string
quartet, plus a Large Ensemble. Piano is a flagship offering, not an add-on — see
`.claude/brand-voice-guidelines.md` § Signature Sound for the full repo cross-reference.

- Say "live piano and strings" or "piano with a string quartet."
- Don't reduce it to "a string quartet" or call it "a band."

## Do

- **Lead with the moment** — anchor copy in a real moment (vow exchange, processional, first
  dance), not features or company history.
- **Use sensory, Bali-specific detail** — cliffside, beach, garden, chapel, golden hour,
  processional, first dance arranged for strings. Doubles as local SEO.
- **Prove credibility with facts** — conservatory-trained musicians, named venues actually played,
  tech rider, planner coordination. If a fact isn't confirmed, leave it out.
- **Match the reader's language** — English first for international couples; natural Bahasa
  Indonesia for domestic couples and local WO. Don't machine-translate or mix mid-sentence.
- **Keep CTAs clear and low-friction** — one primary action per screen ("Check Your Date →"),
  inquiry path two clicks or fewer.
- **Keep the sub-brand link light** — reference Indonesia Istimewa Studio once, quietly (footer
  or one About line). Don't dedicate sections to the parent brand.

## Don't

- **No fabricated data** — never invent prices, travel fees, set durations, deposit terms, dates,
  musician roster, awards, or venue history. Use "pricing on request" and flag for the owner.
- **No academic/sound-art tone bleed** — keep sibling brand Saodor Ensemble's scholarly,
  electroacoustic register ("liminal," "interrogate," "praxis," "electroacoustic transformation")
  out of wedding copy.
- **No heavy jargon for couples** — save technical/formation-spec/tech-rider detail for planner
  pages.
- **No AI-cliché phrasing** — strip "in today's fast-paced world," "delve into," "it's important
  to note," "di era digital saat ini," "tak dapat dipungkiri." Run an Improve Writing pass on
  every draft.
- **No loud/glossy/boho register** — avoid sibling brand FP Music's cinematic-dark tone and any
  cheap-glossy or boho phrasing. Allegra is calm premium: light, airy, refined.
- **No empty superlatives** — avoid "best," "world-class," "paling … di Bali," "number one." Let
  facts and photography carry the claim.
- **No over-claiming, clichés, or empty rhetoric** — state what's true plainly; let concrete
  details and photography carry the message instead of inflated promises.
