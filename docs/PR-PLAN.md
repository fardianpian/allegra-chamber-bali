## Pitch Angle 1 — The Human/Emotion Story

**Best for:** The Lane, Green Wedding Shoes, Rock My Wedding, Junebug Weddings

> **Subject:** Real weddings in Bali: why couples keep choosing live piano for their first dance
>
> Hi [Editor's name],
>
> I run Allegra Chamber Bali — a live chamber ensemble that performs at weddings in Uluwatu, Ubud, and across Bali.
>
> The thing I keep noticing: couples who book us almost always have one specific song they've been listening to together for years — a film score, a pop song, a piece they've never heard played live. We arrange it for piano and strings before the wedding. When we play it at the ceremony or first dance, it's the moment they actually cry.
>
> I'd love to offer a real-wedding feature angle: an international couple, a confirmed Bali venue (we've performed at Alila Villas Uluwatu and Bvlgari Resort Bali), and the story of why they chose live music over a playlist.
>
> We also have a styled shoot proposal ready for submission if that fits your calendar better.
>
> Fardian
> Allegra Chamber Bali
> allegra.indonesiaistimewastudio.id

**Best for:** Condé Nast Traveller, NOW! Bali, Harper's Bazaar Indonesia, The Jakarta Post

> **Subject:** Cliffside wedding music in Uluwatu — what it actually takes
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> A quick pitch for your Bali/destination wedding coverage.
>
> We're Allegra Chamber Bali — a live piano and string ensemble. We perform at cliffside weddings in Uluwatu, including at Alila Villas Uluwatu and Bvlgari Resort Bali, where the ceremony space sits directly above the Indian Ocean.
>
> The challenge nobody talks about: getting a piano, three string players, music stands, and amplification to the edge of a cliff without disrupting the ceremony — and then performing in wind, sun, and humidity while the couple walks in.
>
> I'd offer a 600-word piece or interview on what destination wedding entertainment logistics actually look like in Bali — useful for any couple or planner researching outdoor venues. Could include photography of the setting.
