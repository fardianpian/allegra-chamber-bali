Planning music for an outdoor garden ceremony raises questions a ballroom booking never does — will
strings carry across open lawn, and what happens if it rains? This guide covers formation,
acoustics, and the weather backup plan for a Bali garden wedding, so you can book live music with a
clear picture of how it sounds and runs on the day.

## What Makes Garden Ceremony Music Different From Indoor Venues?

The core difference is that a garden has no walls or ceiling doing the acoustic work a ballroom or
chapel does automatically — sound spreads outward into open air instead of filling a contained
room. That doesn't mean live music sounds worse outdoors; it means formation size, seating
distance, and a weather plan all need to be worked out ahead of time rather than assumed. None of
this is unusual for us — a garden lawn is one of the settings we play most often in Bali.

## How Many Musicians Do You Need for an Outdoor Garden Ceremony?

It depends on your guest count and how far your seating stretches from the musicians, not on the
fact that it's outdoors. A Solo or Duo suits an intimate garden ceremony with close seating, a
Trio or String Quartet fills a wider lawn or a longer program comfortably, and our Large
Ensemble — 4 violins, 2 violas, 2 cellos, double bass, and piano (10 musicians by default) —
carries through a larger garden reception.
Tell us your expected guest count and the shape of your garden layout when you inquire, and we'll
recommend a formation sized to it.

## Does Outdoor Acoustics Change How Piano or Strings Sound?

Strings and piano both still sound like themselves outdoors — what changes is how far that sound
travels before it thins out, which is a question of formation size and seating distance rather
than instrument choice. A close-seated garden ceremony rarely needs amplification at all; a wider
lawn or a larger guest count is where we start the amplification conversation with you. Wind and
ambient garden noise (birdsong, a nearby water feature) are part of the setting, not a problem to
solve — they sit under live acoustic music the same way they would under any outdoor ceremony
sound.

## What's the Best Formation for a Bali Garden Wedding — Piano, Strings, or Both?

There's no single right answer — it comes down to the sound and footprint you want for your
ceremony. Piano is a flagship option in every formation we offer, not a strings-only afterthought,
so the real choice is how you want it used:

| Formation                                                    | Best fit for a garden ceremony                                                                                   |
| ------------------------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Solo Piano                                                   | An intimate ceremony where piano alone carries the processional, vows, and recessional                           |
| Solo Strings (violin or cello)                               | A quiet, close-seated garden ceremony with a single melodic line                                                 |
| Duo (violin, cello & piano)                                  | Piano holding harmony and rhythm while violin and cello carry melody — a fuller sound for a mid-size garden lawn |
| Trio / String Quartet (piano included)                       | The fullest chamber sound for a wider garden layout or a ceremony moving straight into a longer reception        |
| Large Ensemble (strings, double bass & piano — 10 musicians) | A garden reception with dancing or a longer live-music program after the ceremony                                |

If you're drawn to piano's warmth but also want strings carrying the melody, our Duo, Trio, and
String Quartet formations are built for exactly that — piano is included as standard, so you don't
have to choose one instrument over the other.

## What's the Backup Plan If It Rains During a Garden Ceremony?

We agree on a covered backup spot with you before the date, so a rain delay changes where the
music happens, not whether it happens. Most garden venues in Bali already have a veranda, tent, or
indoor room nearby for exactly this reason, and we build that fallback into our planning
conversation with you ahead of time rather than figuring it out on the day. An acoustic piano
needs a covered, weather-protected spot to begin with, which is one more reason we confirm the
backup location as part of booking, not as an afterthought.

## When Should You Book Live Music for a Garden Wedding in Bali?

As soon as your date and venue are confirmed. Garden ceremonies are one of the most-requested
settings for Bali weddings, so the formations and dates couples want most during peak wedding
season are also the ones that fill up furthest in advance. Booking early also gives us more time
to plan around your specific garden layout, guest count, and any first-dance or custom arrangement
you want built into the program.
