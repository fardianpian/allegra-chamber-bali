A wedding pianist brings the same chamber-music intimacy as a string ensemble, built around a
single instrument that can carry both harmony and melody on its own — making it one of the most
flexible ways to bring live music into a Bali ceremony, from a quiet garden vow exchange to a full
reception.

## What a Wedding Pianist Covers on the Day

At Allegra Chamber Bali, Solo Piano is one of our Solo formation options, alongside violin and
cello — not a cheaper substitute for strings. A solo pianist can perform the full arc of a
ceremony: a prelude as guests arrive, the processional, music during the signing or vow exchange,
and the recessional as the couple leaves. The same pianist can continue into cocktail hour or a
reception set, with the program built around the moments you tell us matter most.

Every piece is arranged in advance around your ceremony order and the songs you want included —
classical repertoire, a film or pop arrangement, or a custom version of a song that means something
to your story, such as a first dance.

## Solo Piano or a String Quartet?

Both are live chamber music, but they read differently in the room. A string quartet fills a space
with four independent voices — useful for a larger venue or a fuller reception sound. A solo
pianist is quieter and more intimate: one instrument, holding harmony and melody at once, well
suited to a smaller ceremony, an indoor venue, or a couple who want the music to feel personal
rather than grand.

Neither is "better" — it comes down to your venue, guest count, and the feeling you want. If you're
unsure, tell us your venue and ceremony size and we'll recommend a formation.

## Piano Within a Larger Formation

Piano doesn't have to stand alone. It's a flagship instrument across every Allegra formation, not
an optional add-on:

- Piano is **included as standard**, not a paid upgrade, within our Duo, Trio, and String Quartet
  packages — piano holds the harmony and rhythm while the strings carry the melody.
- Our **Large Ensemble** — 4 violins, 2 violas, 2 cellos, double bass, and piano (10 musicians by
  default) — already includes piano in its default lineup, for couples who want the fullest sound
  across ceremony and reception.

This means choosing a wedding pianist doesn't lock you out of strings later, and choosing strings
doesn't mean leaving piano out — the two are designed to work together.

## What to Expect When You Book

Our musicians are conservatory-trained and perform without backing tracks — every note on the day
is played live in the room. For outdoor venues (cliffside, beach, or garden ceremonies, which are
common across Bali), we just need shade for the musician and a flat, stable surface for the piano
or keyboard; let us know your venue when you inquire and we'll confirm what's needed.
