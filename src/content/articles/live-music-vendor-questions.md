Booking live music for a client's wedding means vetting a vendor you may never meet before the
day itself — and the wrong questions leave logistics undiscovered until it's too late to fix.
This guide walks through what to ask, with Allegra Chamber Bali's own answers to each one, so you
can judge any live ensemble against a clear standard.

## Can They Share a Tech Rider and Stage Plot Before You Book?

Yes, and you should ask for one before confirming any live ensemble, not after. A stage plot
shows where each musician and instrument sits, and an input list and power requirements let your
sound or venue team plan around it instead of improvising on the day. At Allegra Chamber Bali,
these documents are available on request for every formation — see [For Planners](/for-planners/)
or message us directly and we'll send them over.

## What's Their Setup Routine on the Wedding Day?

A reliable vendor arrives early enough that setup is finished, not still happening, when the
first guest sits down. Our musicians arrive ahead of schedule, set up quietly, and follow a
run-of-show that's been confirmed with you in advance — so there's nothing for you to manage on
site beyond the cues you've already agreed on.

## How Fast Do They Respond, and Who's Your Point of Contact?

Ask for a single point of contact, not a different person each time you follow up. We respond to
planner and venue inquiries within 24 hours and keep one contact across confirmations, changes,
and day-of questions, so a timeline update doesn't get lost between people.

## Can They Adapt to Outdoor or Non-Standard Venues?

Yes — cliffside, beach, and garden ceremonies are part of our regular booking calendar across
Bali, and a vendor who's done this before should be able to tell you exactly what the venue
requires, not just agree to perform there. Setup stays simple: shade for the musicians and a
flat, stable surface for instruments. Tell us your venue type when you inquire and we'll confirm
what's needed ahead of time.

## How Do They Handle Custom Song Requests and Arrangements?

A live ensemble worth booking should be able to take a couple's own song list and arrange it for
the formation they've chosen, not just play from a fixed setlist. Our musicians are
conservatory-trained and perform without backing tracks, and most requests — classical, film, or
pop — can be arranged for strings, piano, or both. Browse [Repertoire](/repertoire/) for
examples, or send a couple's song list once a formation is confirmed.

## What Should You Confirm Before Locking In a Date?

Confirm the date and formation as early as possible — popular dates in Bali book out 6 to 12
months ahead, especially during peak wedding season, so an early hold protects the date even
before every other detail is settled. Pricing isn't published, since it depends on formation,
sets, and travel area; we'll follow up with a personalised quote once you reach out with your
client's date and venue.
